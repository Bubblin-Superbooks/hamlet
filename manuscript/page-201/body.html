FIRST CLOWN. Mine, sir. [Sings.] O, a pit of clay for to be made For such a guest is meet. HAMLET. I think it be thine indeed, for thou liest in’t. FIRST CLOWN. You lie out on’t, sir, and therefore ’tis not yours. For my part, I do not lie in’t, yet it is mine. HAMLET. Thou dost lie in’t, to be in’t and say it is thine. ’Tis for the dead, not for the quick; therefore thou liest.

FIRST CLOWN. ’Tis a quick lie, sir; ’t will away again from me to you.

HAMLET. What man dost thou dig it for? FIRST CLOWN. For no man, sir.

HAMLET. What woman then? FIRST CLOWN. For none neither.

HAMLET. Who is to be buried in’t? FIRST CLOWN. One that was a woman, sir; but, rest her soul, she’s dead.
