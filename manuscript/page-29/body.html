Enter Horatio, Marcellus and Barnardo.

HORATIO. Hail to your lordship! HAMLET. I am glad to see you well: Horatio, or I do forget myself. HORATIO. The same, my lord, And your poor servant ever.

HAMLET. Sir, my good friend; I’ll change that name with you: And what make you from Wittenberg, Horatio?— Marcellus? MARCELLUS. My good lord. HAMLET. I am very glad to see you.—Good even, sir.— But what, in faith, make you from Wittenberg? HORATIO. A truant disposition, good my lord. HAMLET.
