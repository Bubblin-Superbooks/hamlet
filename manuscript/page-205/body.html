HORATIO. What’s that, my lord? HAMLET. Dost thou think Alexander looked o’ this fashion i’ th’earth? HORATIO. E’en so. HAMLET. And smelt so? Pah! [Throws down the skull.]

HORATIO. E’en so, my lord. HAMLET. To what base uses we may return, Horatio! Why may not imagination trace the noble dust of Alexander till he find it stopping a bung-hole?

HORATIO. ’Twere to consider too curiously to consider so. HAMLET. No, faith, not a jot. But to follow him thither with modesty enough, and likelihood to lead it; as thus.
